## **C.E.T.R.**

A simple, powerful loop for learning how to learn.

<div class="columns">
<div>

###  Conjecture
**"I have an idea!"**
A child forms their own bold theory about how something works.

### Explanation
**"Here's *why* I think it works..."**
They move beyond simple description to articulating a causal mechanism.

</div>
<div>

### Testing
**"Let's try it!"**
They test their idea against reality. Reality, not an adult, is the judge.

### Refinement
**"My idea was wrong/incomplete. How can I make it better?"**
They improve their idea based on the evidence.

</div>
</div>

![bg right:40%](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1608109199974-81c47a54b3a4?ixlib=rb-4.0.3&ixid=MnwxMjA3fDB8MHxwaG90by1wYWdlfHx8fGVufDB8fHx8&auto=format&fit=crop&w=1770&q=80)

1.  **Conjecture:** "A wider base will make it more stable!"

2.  **Explanation:** "Because the weight is spread out, so it won't tip over."

3.  **Testing:** Build several towers with different bases and see which ones withstand a "shake test."

4.  **Refinement:** "It's not just the base, but the *ratio* of the base to the height that matters!"

**Outcome: A deep, intuitive grasp of physics, discovered through play.**
